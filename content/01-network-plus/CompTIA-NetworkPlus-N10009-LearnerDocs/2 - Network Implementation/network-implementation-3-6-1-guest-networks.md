1. Guest networks 

Learner Objective: *Learn about the use of guest networks in wireless environments*    

Description: In this episode, you will learn about the use of guest networks in modern wireless environments. This includes the use of captive portals.  

-----------

* Guest networks in wireless environments serve as separate, isolated networks that provide internet access to visitors or guests while maintaining the security and integrity of the main network. 
* This feature is commonly found in home Wi-Fi routers, business networks, and public Wi-Fi hotspots.
* Guest networks are designed to be isolated from the main network to prevent unauthorized access to sensitive information and devices. Devices connected to the guest network typically cannot communicate with devices on the primary network.
* Guests are granted access to the internet but are restricted from accessing local resources, such as printers, shared files, or other devices on the main network. This helps maintain the privacy and security of the main network.
* A captive portal is a web page that guests encounter when connecting to a guest network. Before gaining full access to the internet, users are required to authenticate or agree to terms and conditions through the captive portal. This authentication process helps ensure that only authorized users connect to the guest network.
* Captive portals may employ various authentication methods, including:
Open Access: Guests can connect without a password, but they must often agree to terms of service or provide some basic information. Password Protection: Guests need a password to access the network. Voucher Systems: Users may receive a voucher or temporary access code for a limited duration. Social Media Login: Guests can log in using their social media credentials.
