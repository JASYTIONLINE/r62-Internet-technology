1. Installation implications 

Learner Objective: *Learn about various physical installation factors and implications*    

Description: In this episode, you will learn about various implications when it comes to physical installations. These include installation locations, rack sizes, port-side exhaust/intakes, cabling, and lockable devices.   

-----------

* Locations for physical installation components include: 
 - Main Distribution Frame (MDF) - the central point where the core network equipment is located
 - Intermediate Distribution Frame (IDF) - these are intermediate points between the MDF and the end-user locations; these are strategically placed to minimize cable lengths 
 - Wiring closets - physical spaces in buildings where network cabling is terminated 
 - Patch panels - these are mounted in IDFs or wiring closets and serve as termination points for horizontal cabling 
 - Fiber distribution panel (FDP) - a centralized unit designed for the termination, organization, and management of fiber optic cables. It plays a crucial role in ensuring efficient connectivity, scalability, and maintenance of fiber optic infrastructure. The FDP is typically located in a data center, communication room, or telecommunications closet. 
 - Cable pathways - these are conduits and trays that are used to route and manage cables between the MDF, IDFs, and end users 
* Rack sizes is another major consideration with physical installations - these considerations include: 
 - Rack height, rack width, rack depth, airflow and cooling, cable management, expansion and scalability, security and accessibility (lockable), weight capacity 
* Port-side exhaust/intake consideration in physical IT installations refers to the orientation of equipment within racks in relation to the placement of exhaust and intake ports. Proper consideration of this aspect is crucial for optimizing cooling efficiency and preventing overheating in data centers and server rooms.
