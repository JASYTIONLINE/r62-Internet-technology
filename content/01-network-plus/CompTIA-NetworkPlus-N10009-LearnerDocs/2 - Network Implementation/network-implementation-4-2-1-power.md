1. Power

Learner Objective: *Learn about common power considerations for physical IT installations*    

Description: In this episode, you will learn about power considerations for physical installations including, Uninterruptible Power Supplies (UPS), power distribution units (PDUs), power loads, and voltages.   

-----------

* Uninterruptible Power Supply (UPS) is an essential device in information technology (IT) infrastructure designed to provide temporary power during electrical outages, fluctuations, or disruptions. The UPS ensures that critical IT equipment, such as servers, networking devices, and storage systems, can continue to operate seamlessly and avoid data loss or downtime.
* A Power Distribution Unit (PDU) is a device designed to distribute electrical power to multiple information technology (IT) equipment, such as servers, networking devices, and storage systems. PDUs play a crucial role in data centers, server rooms, and other IT installations, ensuring efficient power distribution, monitoring, and management.
* Power load refers to the amount of electrical power consumed by the various IT equipment and devices within a specific location, such as a data center, server room, or network closet. Understanding and managing power load is crucial for ensuring the stability, reliability, and efficiency of the IT infrastructure.
* Voltage is a fundamental electrical property that plays a crucial role in modern IT installations. It is a measure of the electric potential difference between two points in an electrical circuit and is expressed in volts (V). In the context of a modern IT installation, understanding voltage is essential for ensuring the proper functioning, safety, and efficiency of electronic equipment.
