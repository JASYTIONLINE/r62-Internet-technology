--------  

* What are some of the common cabling tools?
  + Cable cutter
    - A tool used to cut through various types of cables, including electrical wires and optical fibers, for precise cut for installations or maintenance work.
  + Cable stripper
    - A tool designed to remove the insulation from electrical wires and optical fibers without damaging the conductor or fiber inside.
  + Cable crimper
    - A tool used to join connector ends to various types of cables, including:
      * Coaxial
      * Ethernet
      * Fiber optics
  + Punchdown tool
    - A tool used to insert and secure wires into punchdown blocks or keystone modules.
* How can we ensure the cables are working properly?
   + Cable Tester
    - Verifies the electrical connections in a signal cable or other wired assembly, testing for continuity, resistance, and ensuring no shorts or open circuits.
  + Visual Fault Locator
    - Emits a red laser light to visually identify breaks, bends, or faults in fiber optic cables, aiding in troubleshooting and maintenance.
  + Optical Time-Domain Reflectometers (OTDRs)
    - are diagnostic tools used to characterize optical fibers by measuring light reflections and losses, identifying faults, and determining fiber length and splice locations.
* What if you need to locating cables in a build?
  + Tone generator and locator
    - A device used to trace and identify wires or cables in a bundle, ensuring proper routing and connectivity.
* How about monitoring data on a cable?
  + Network tap
    - A tool that allow monitoring of network traffic transparently, enabling data capture without interrupting the flow.
