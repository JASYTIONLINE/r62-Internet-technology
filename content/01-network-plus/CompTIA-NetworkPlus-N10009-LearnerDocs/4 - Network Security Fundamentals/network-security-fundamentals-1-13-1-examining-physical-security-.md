## 2-4-1: Examining Physical Security

At the end of this episode, I will be able to:    

1. Identify and explain the importance of physical security, given a scenario.    

Learner Objective: *Identify and explain the importance of physical security, given a scenario.*    

Description: In this episode, the learner will examine the importance of physical security to protect assets. We will explore common methods used when implementing physical security infrastructure. 

--------  


* What does physical security mean in terms of I.T.?
	- Involves implementing controls to protect the physical infrastructure, hardware, and sensitive data, aiming to protect the tangible components.
* What are some common physical security controls?
	+ Bollards - provides barriers against vehicle-based attacks to prevent access to sensitive areas
	+ Access control vestibule - creates a controlled entry point, mitigating the risk of unauthorized access to secure locations
	+ Fencing - establishes clear boundaries and a visible and physical barrier that protects assets, facilities, and personnel
	+ Video surveillance - enables real-time monitoring and deterrence of potential threats and rapid response to security incidents by providing visual oversight of areas
	+ Security guard - provides a human presence, deterring potential threats, conducting proactive monitoring, and responding to incidents.
	+ Access badge -  enables controlled entry, authentication, and tracking of personnel within a facility, maintaining security and accountability
	+ Lighting - enhances visibility, reduces hiding spots and deters potential intruders
	+ Sensors - providing real-time detection and alerts for unauthorized movements, breaches, and environmental changes, providing early threat identification and response
		- Sensor types
			* Infrared - detect heat signatures and movement in \(low-light conditions\)
			* Pressure - detects changes in pressure caused by \(human or vehicular movement\)
			* Microwave - emit microwave signals to detect motion across \(various environments and conditions\)
			* Ultrasonic - emitting high-frequency sound waves to detect movement and proximity \(indoor spaces\)
